Prefixes

See list of all Medical Terminology/Cheat Sheet and their definitions.

Directions & Positions

ab-

away from

Example: abduction (process of drawing away from)

ad-

toward

Example: adduction (process of drawing toward)

ante-

before

Example: antenatal (pertaining to before birth; the fetus; prenatal)

circum-

around

Example: circumduction (process of drawing a part in a circular motion)

dia-

through; complete

Example: diagnosis (the identification of an illness)

ecto-

outside

Example: ectogenous (produced from the outside)

endo-

within

Example: endoscope (instrument used to visually examine a body cavity or
organ)

epi-

upon; on; above

Example: epigastric (pertaining to upon the stomach)

e-

out; outward; outside

Example: eversion (process of turning out/outward)

ex-

out; outward; outside

Example: excision (process of cutting out)

exo-

out; outward; outside

extra-

out; outward; outside

Example: extraocular (pertaining to outside the eye)

hyper-

excessive; above

Example: hyperplasia (increased cell production)

hypo-

below; under; deficient

Example: hypogastric (pertaining to below the stomach)

in-

in; into

Example: incision (process of cutting into)

infra-

below; beneath

Example: infracostal (pertaining to below the ribs)

inter-

between

Example: intercellular (pertaining to between the cells)

intra-

within

Example: intracranial (pertaining to within the skull)

meta-

beyond

Example: metastasis (the uncontrolled spread of cancerous cells beyond the original tumor site)

para-

beside; near

Example: paranasal (pertaining to near the nose)

per-

through

Example: percutaneous (pertaining to through the skin)

peri-

around

Example: perineuritis (inflammation around a nerve)

post-

after

Example: postmortem (after death)

pre-

before

Example: prenatal (pertaining to before birth; the fetus; antenatal)

pro-

before

Example: prodrome (symptom/s occurring before the onset of disease)

retro-

back; behind

Example: retroversion (backward turning or tipping of an organ)

sub-

under; below

Example: sublingual (pertaining to under the tongue)

supra-

above

Example: suprarenal (pertaining to above the kidney)

trans-

across

Example: transection (process of cutting across)

ultra-

beyond

Example: ultrasonography (process of using high-frequency sound waves to create images of internal organs; ultrasound)

Negative

anti-

against

Example: antibiotic (drugs used to kill harmful bacteria)

a-

no; not; lack of

Example: aseptic (no contamination)

an-

no; not; lack of

Example: anemia (lack of red blood cells)

contra-

against; opposite

Example: contralateral (pertaining to the opposite side)

in-

not

Example: indigestible (not capable of being digested)

Numbers

bi-

two

Example: bilateral (pertaining to two sides)

di-

two

Example: dissection (to cut into two pieces

hemi-

half

Example: hemigastrectomy (excision of half the stomach)

semi-

half

Example: semicomatose (a state of unconsciousness where the person can be roused)

mono-

one

Example: monocyte (immune cell with a single nucleus)

uni-

one

Example: unilateral (pertaining to one side)

multi-

many

Example: multiform (having many shapes)

poly-

many

Example: polyadenoma (tumour of many glands)

tri-

three

Example: tricuspid (three cusps/projections)

tetra-

four

Example: tetraplegia (paralysis of all four limbs; quadriplegia)

quadri-

four

Example: quadrilateral (pertaining to four sides)

Additional

ana-

apart; up

auto-

self

brady-

slow

Example: bradycardia (pertaining to a slow heartbeat)

dys-

bad; abnormal; difficult; pain

Example: dysplasia (abnormal development)

macro-

large

Example: macrocephalia (excessively large head)

mal-

bad

Example: malaise (a bad feeling; a sign of illness)

micro-

small

neo-

new

pan-

all

Example: panhysterectomy (excision of all the uterus)

syn-

together; with; joined

Example: synarthrotic (a type of joint in which the bones are joined)

sym-

together; with; joined

tachy-

fast; rapid

Example: tachycardia (pertaining to fast heartbeat of over 100 beats per minute)
