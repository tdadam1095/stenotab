Sources of Law

1. Constitutional Law

  • The highest law (all other laws have to follow it)
  • The Canadian Constitution:
    • Sets out the division of powers (different levels of government have authority to pass laws)
    • Contains the Charter of Rights and Freedoms (outlines the rights of individuals that the government cannot infringe upon within reasonable limits)
      • Fundamental freedoms
      • Democratic rights
      • Mobility rights
      • Legal rights
      • Equality rights
      • Language rights

2. Statute Law

  • Made by elected lawmakers ("government-made law")
  • A law becomes statute when it's passed (e.g., driving age)

3. Common Law / Case Law

  • Based on past court decisions
  • Decisions/rulings made by judges can set precedents, but it is flexible; judges can set new legal doctrines
  • Stare decisis: the rule of precedent
    • Lower courts must follow the decisions of higher courts (decisions are binding)
    • Courts of equal rank should try to follow decisions (decisions are persuasive)
Public & private law in Canada

All of Canada follows the same public law (based on English law)

Quebec follows different private law (i.e., statute and common law) in the Civil Code of Quebec
