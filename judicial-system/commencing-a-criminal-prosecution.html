Commencing a Criminal Prosecution

Charging Documents

  • Information
    • Charging document used in Provincial Court
    • Prepared by a police officer
    • Inform the accused of the allegations against them
  • Indictment
    • Charging document used in Court of King's Bench
    • Prepared by the Crown prosecutor
    • Informs the accused of the allegations against them
  • Each separate charge on either an Information or an Indictment is a count

Endorsements

  • A point-form summary of court proceedings for each hearing date
    • Date, time, and courtroom number
    • Judge, counsel, court reporter, and court clerk
    • Relevant events
    • Disposition/adjournment date
  • Typically prepared by the court clerk

Courts of Criminal Jurisdiction

  • Trial courts
    • Provincial Court
    • Court of King's Bench
  • Appellate courts
    • Court of King's Bench
    • Court of Appeal
    • Supreme Court of Canada

Criminal Offence Classifications

  • Determined by looking up the offence in the Criminal Code and reading the section

  • Hybrid offence (aka dual procedure offence) is decided by Crown's election based on:
    • Circumstances of the case
    • Prior criminal record
    • Public interest
    • Impact on victims (testify twice, long delay, etc.)
Classifications of common offences

Indictable offences: murder, robbery

Summary conviction offences: causing a disturbance

Hybrid offence: sexual interference, theft (indictable if stolen goods worth >$5,000; summary conviction if worth <$5,000)

Proceeding by Indictment

  • Accused's election: the accused is given an option as to what level of court they would like to be tried by:
    • A Provincial Court judge
    • A Court of King's Bench justice
    • A Court of King's Bench justice with a jury

Summary of Elections

Who has the election? When? Options
Crown Accused is charged with a hybrid offence Proceed by:
1) Indictment
2) Summarily
Accused Accused if charged with an indictable offence Trial by:
1) PC judge
2) KB justice (with a preliminary in PC)
3) KB justice and jury (with a preliminary in PC)

Absolute Jurisdiction

  • If a Court has absolute jurisdiction, the accused does not get an election even if the offence is indictable or hybrid and the Crown proceeds by Indictment
    • Section 533 trials must be in Provincial Court
    • Section 469 trials must be in Court of King's Bench with a jury
