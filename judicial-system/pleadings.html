Pleadings

Civil Claims

  • Lawsuits between individuals and/or businesses to address
    • Disputes arising from contracts
    • OR claims for injury to a person, their property, or their reputation

Alberta Rules of Court

  • Sets out the procedures and documentation which govern civil actions in Alberta
    • Helps parties identify issues in dispute
    • Encourages parties to resolve claims on their own
    • Requires parties to communicate honestly and openly
    • Ensure lawsuit progresses in a timely manner
  • For court reporters, it sets out the duties, responsibilities, and tariff of fees for court-generated transcripts

Parties to a Lawsuit

  1. Plaintiff
  2. Defendant
  3. Plaintiff by counterclaim
  4. Defendant by counterclaim
  5. Third party defendant
  6. Amicus curiae (ad litem)
  7. Petitioner/respondent
  8. Applicant/respondent

Stages of a Civil Litigation

  1. Pleadings
  2. Discovery/disclosure
  3. Trial

Pleadings

  • Formal documents used to initiate, defend, and advance a lawsuit
  • Must be filed at the courthouse in the appropriate judicial center
  • Service: formal process by which a party named in a lawsuit receives a copy of the pleadings; the Rules of Court outline how and when
    • Personal service
    • Substitutional service
    • Service by mail
    • Service by email
    • Service ex juris

Statement of Claim

  • Issued by the plaintiff to the defendant
  • Used to initiate litigation
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Demand for Notice

  • Issued by the defendant to the plaintiff
  • Used to acknowledge a Statement of Claim
  • Seeks notice of further steps in the litigation

Statement of Defence

  • Issued by the defendant to the plaintiff
  • Used to dispute a Statement of Claim
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Counterclaim

  • Issued by the defendant to the plaintiff in conjunction with the Statement of Defence
  • Used to commence a claim against the plaintiff in response to the original claim
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Statement of Defence to Counterclaim

  • Issued by the plaintiff to the defendant
  • Used to dispute a Counterclaim
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Third Party Claim

  • Issued by the defendant to the third party
  • Used to make a claim against a non-party
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Third Party Statement of Defence

  • Issued by the third party to the plaintiff and defendant
  • Used to dispute a Third Party Claim
  • Contains the "facts" relied upon
  • Identifies the remedy sought

Additional Pleadings

Request for Particulars

  • May be issued by any party
  • Used to request further info in order to properly respond to a pleading

Reply to Defence / Reply to Defence of Third Party Defendant

  • Issued by the plaintiff
  • Optional pleading used to respond to issues newly raised in a defence

Close of Pleadings

  • Pleadings close when
    • A reply is filled
    • OR the time for filing a reply expires (whichever is earlier)

Noting in Default

  1. Defendant fails to issue a Statement of Defence
  2. Plaintiff requests a Noting in Default
  3. Court assesses quantum of damages rather than liability
  4. Judgment is issued

Categorizing Litigation

  • Standard or complex based on
    • Amount of claim
    • Number of parties and documents
    • Number and complexity of issues
    • Length of time questioning will take
    • Whether expert reports are required
    • Whether a third party claim will likely be made

Complex Litigation

  • Parties must file a litigation plan with the court
    • Early planning and preparing for each stage of the litigation
    • Completion dates for
      • Identification of the issues in dispute
      • Disclosure of records
      • Questioning of parties
      • Exchanging of expert reports
      • Applying for a trial date
  • Parties must monitor progress and adjust dates as needed
  • A Court of King's Bench justice may be assigned as case manager to keep the matter moving forward
