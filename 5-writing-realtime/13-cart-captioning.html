CART Captioning

Intro

  • display options may include computers, monitors, large screens, and mobile devices
  • we can share our speech to text display anywhere
  • many web conferencing platforms allow captioners to provide captions for online meetings (host must assign closed captioning privileges to the captioner)
  • one to one captioner to client experience is when a captioner sits next to the client and shares a display with the client (choose a clean layout - no line numbers, normal view, hidden menu bars, customize color, font style, and font size)

Output to CARTView

What is it?

  • An alternative to sharing the writer’s screen is Output to CARTView which allows the writer to display the text on a second monitor or screen.
  • CARTView (local display) is available in the Output To: field in the Translate dialog box. Outputting to CARTView displays a separate window which can display on your computer or you can drag the window to another monitor or computer screen. The CARTView window displays text with no toolbars or menus.
  • The display may be customized by changing the color of the text, background, font style and size, background transparency, line spacing and the position of the window. You can save the CARTView settings to the Alternate User Settings feature.
  • Editing changes to the realtime screen are reflected on the CARTView screen. If the CARTView window is displayed on another monitor/computer, you can use editing features that display dialog boxes on the realtime screen without displaying the dialog boxes on the CARTView window. For example, you can use the Power Define feature to easily define from your writing machine or you can have the Brief It pane open in your software.
  • The Case CATalyst CARTView Settings Icon is available in the Windows System Tray. When selected, the icon displays the CARTView screen settings such as font, transparency, color, etc. The system tray icon makes it easy to control the CARTView settings when you do not have instant access to the CARTView window.
  • For example, let’s say you are in the back of a large convention room with CARTView displaying on a large screen in the front of the room when you realize you need to change the color or the size of the text to make it more readable. By clicking the Case CATalyst CARTView Settings icon, you will instantly have access to the CARTView settings. No need to go to the front of the room to make adjustments.

Create Parentheticals

What is it?

  • Parentheticals are commonplace in CART and should be used to indicate environmental sounds that may or may not be apparent to the consumer.

Style

  • Parentheticals should appear in title case with an open bracket, space, \<text>, space, close bracket.

Defined in the dictionary as:

  1. \<Parenthetical>
  2. (
  3. \<Sticky Space>
  4. \<Caption: KeepCaseOn>
  5. Cheers and Applause
  6. \<Caption: KeepCaseOff>
  7. \<Sticky Space>
  8. )
  • A parenthetical should appear independently on a new line
  • No punctuation is applied to the parenthetical
  • Note: The ( Inaudible ) parenthetical is used when the writer cannot hear what has been said and stays in line with the text
