Joanne's Tips!

The software we use in the program doesn't need any special requirements for a laptop. The minimum requirements recommended by Stenograph are included below. However, most laptops on sale now will meet or exceed them.

Some advice as you begin your search:

  • Set a budget and buy the most RAM and hard drive space available within your budget.
  • A solid-state drive (SSD) is recommended over an HDD, but either one will suit your needs.
  • Consider the weight of the laptop as you will be carrying is around when you start working. You can purchase an external monitor and wireless keyboard/mouse for use at home to increase your workspace.
  • Keyboard and touchpad - try it before you buy it! Type on it and see how it feels, if it's noisy, and whether your finger can easily reach the enter key. Some keyboards have the enter key a little farther away which makes it harder to reach.
  • A minimum of three UDB ports. If the laptop you like has less than three, you may want to purchase a USB hub. The ports are needed to hook up various devices to your computer.

Case CATalyst

  • Operating System
    • Windows 10
    • Processor speed of 2GHz or higher
    • intel i3, i5, i7, or AMD equivalent is strongly recommended
  • RAM (Memory)
    • 4 GB of RAM or more is required if using the RealTeam feature
  • Hard Drive
    • 40 GB hard drive with at least 20 GB of free space
    • 80 GB hard drive or larger is recommended
  • Video Card
    • A DirectX 9 compatible video card with a minimum of 256 MB Video RAM.
  • Display
    • SVGA monitor capable with a 1024x768 or high resolution.
  • Audio Card
    • Windows compatible sound card for audio recording at 8KHz, 16-bit
  • Microphone
    • Windows compatible external, self-powered/battery-powered microphone
  • Ports
    • USB port for the writing machine to write realtime, update the writing machine dictionary, and update the writing machine software

Dress for Success

Your clothes should

  • complement your personal coloring and body type
  • be of classic, timeless styling
  • coordinate in color, fabric and pattern
  • fit comfortably and be well-fitted
  • be of the best quality you can afford
  • be appropriate for the occasion and current for the times

Business Appropriate Basics:

  • Always observe the guidelines for proper fit and personal grooming
  • Choose classic styles over the latest fashion
  • All garments should be well-pressed, with no stains or tears
  • Details count - accessories, jewellery, et cetera
  • Meet client expectations
  • Dress consistently
    Women:
  • Women are recommended to always wear a jacket. It gives you more presence and takes up more space. It gives a look of credibility and authority.
  • Make sure you are covered up appropriately, including bending over, et cetera.
  • Skirt level should be somewhere around your knee.
  • It is recommended women’s shoes should have a closed toe and a heel. Flat shoes can unintentionally deflate a strong image.
  • All garments should be well-pressed, no stains or tears.
  • Nails should be manicured.
  • Dark clothing should be brushed with a lint brush.
  • Hair should be well-managed and controlled.
  • Make-up, strive for a natural look for business.
  • Discard dated clothing and accessories. Update eyewear.
    Men:
  • Do not forget to shave and comb your hair.
  • Pay attention to detail. Make sure your socks are up and shoes are shined.
  • Men should always wear a collar.
  • Men should always wear a jacket on the job.
  • The tie should touch the belt buckle.
  • The pants should kiss the shoes.
  • Beware of breath.
    Dressing for Court & Questionings/Hearings = Business professional
  • muted colors
  • closed-toe shoes
  • jacket or structured cardigan
  • piercings and tattoos not visible
  • sleeves
  • tie and jacket for men
  • clothing should not draw attention to yourself
  • nothing controversial
    Dressing for CART (post-secondary setting or meetings) = business casual

****2313 module 3 hardware

  • LAN / Local Area Network
    • A computer network limited to a small area which makes it easy for users to share info
  • Router
    • Device that allows multiple computers to join the same network
    • Early versions had Ethernet ports for wired connections
    • Modern versions offer wireless connectivity
    • Connecting to a router provides access to a LAN, it does not necessarily provide access to the Internet
      • The router must be connected to a modem to connect to the Internet
  • Modem
    • Device that provides access to the Internet
      • By connecting the modem to the router (instead of directly to a computer), all devices connected to the router can access the modem and, therefore, the Internet
