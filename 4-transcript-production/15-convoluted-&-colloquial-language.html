Convoluted and Colloquial Language

Thinking Sounds

  • Omit filler sounds (e.g., "uh," "m-hm") unless crucial for context
Incorrect

Q Do you remember what happened?

A Uh. Not very well.

Correct

Q Do you remember what happened?

A Not very well.

  • Recognize meaningful mumbles (e.g., "uh-huh" for yes)
    • m-hm
    • uh-huh (affirmative)
    • uh-uh (negative)
    • hmm (pensive)
  • Differentiate between “verbatim” and excessive repetition or stuttering

Convoluted Sentence Structure

  • Look at context to clarify meaning
  • Use punctuation to enhance readability, like internal commas for lists or appositives
Incorrect

... where a post office employee -- a post master -- works ...

Correct

... where a post office employee, a post master, works ...

Finding Sentences

  • Identify cohesive thoughts
  • Use periods for clarity
  • Use paragraphs to convey new topics

Word Choices

  • Kindness over correctness
  • If a word choice will embarrass the speaker, don't use it
Incorrect

gonna
'cause
should of

Correct

going to
because
should have

Thought Process vs. Conversation

  • Quote thoughts or dialogue as they would be expressed in conversation

Dashes

  • Don't start a line with a dash
  • Don’t use dashes with complete thoughts
Incorrect

Q Were you --
A I don't know.
Q -- were you in attendance on that day?

Correct

Q Were you --
A I don't know.
Q Were you in attendance on that day?

Research

  • Use relevant words/phrases to find correct spellings when unsure

Formatting

  • Keep related items (names, dates, addresses) together on the same line
Sometimes it's just not possible to make something read well
