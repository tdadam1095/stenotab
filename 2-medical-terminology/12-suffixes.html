Suffixes

See list of all 14 Cheat Sheet and their definitions.

General

-cyte

cell

Example: adipocyte (fat cell)

-er

specialist

Example: practitioner (one who specializes in some practice/discipline)

-ician

specialist

Example: physician (one who specializes in medicine)

-(o)logist

specialist

Example: cardiologist (one who specializes in the study/treatment of the heart)

-ist

specialist

Example: pharmacist (one who specializes in drugs)

-ion

process

Example: section (process of cutting)

-(o)logy

study of

Example: cardiology (study of the heart)

-or

"one who"

Example: donor (one who donates)

-plasia

development

Example: dysplasia (abnormal development)

-poiesis

production

Example: cytopoiesis (production of cells)

Descriptive

-genic

producing

Example: carcinogenic (something that produces cancer)

-oid

resembling

Example: osteoid (resembling bone)

-ole

small

Example: bronchiole (small bronchus)

-ule

small

Example: venule (small vein)

-ac

pertaining to

Example: cardiac (pertaining to the heart)

-al

pertaining to

Example: renal (pertaining to the kidney)

-ary

pertaining to

Example: mammary (pertaining to the breast)

-eal

pertaining to

Example: pharyngeal (pertaining to the throat)

-ic

pertaining to

Example: gastric (pertaining to the stomach)

-ous

pertaining to

Example: venous (pertaining to a vein)

Pathologic Conditions

-rrhage

bursting forth

Example: hemorrhage (bleeding)

-rrhagia

bursting forth

Example: gastrorrhagia (bleeding from the stomach)

-rrhea

flow; discharge

Example: otorrhea (discharge from the ear)

-rrhexis

rupture

Example: splenorrhexis (ruptured spleen)

-sclerosis

hardening

Example: arteriosclerosis (hardening of the arteries)

-spasm

sudden involuntary contraction

Example: blepharospasm (involuntary contraction of the eyelid)

-stenosis

narrowing

Example: phlebostenosis (narrowing of the vein)

-y

process

Example: neuropathy (disease process of the nerve)

Diagnostic Procedures

-gram

record; written record or image

Example: mammogram (record of the breast)

-graph

instrument used to record

Example: cardiograph (instrument used to record heart activity)

(o)graphy

process of recording/producing images

Example 1: computed tomography (CT scan; imaging method that uses x-rays to create pictures of cross-sections of the body)

Example 2: mammography (producing images of the breast)

-meter

instrument used to measure

Example: craniometer (instrument used to measure the skull)

-metry

to measure; measurement

Example: pelvimetry (measurement of the pelvis)

-opsy

to view

Example: biopsy (a procedure that removes a small sample of tissue for microscopic examination to check for abnormalities)

-scope

instrument used to visually examine a body cavity or organ

Example: bronchoscope (instrument used to visually examine the inside of the bronchus)

-(o)scopy

process of visually examining a body cavity or organ

Example 1: fluoroscopy (x-ray of moving structures, like substances through the digestive tract)

Example 2: bronchoscopy (process of visually examining the bronchus)

Surgical Procedures

-centesis

surgical puncture to remove fluid

Example: abdominocentesis (surgical puncture to remove fluid from the abdomen)

-desis

surgical binding/fusion

Example: arthrodesis (surgical fusion of the joint)

-ectomy

excision; surgical removal

Example: tonsillectomy (excision of the tonsils)

-pexy

surgical fixation (to fasten in a fixed position; stabilize)

Example: nephropexy (surgical fixation of the kidney)

-plasty

surgical reconstruction/repair

Example: orchidoplasty (surgical reconstruction of the testicle)

-rrhaphy

to suture (to sew)

Example: colporrhaphy (suturing of the vagina)

-stasis

stoppage; controlling

Example: hemostasis (stoppage of blood)

-stomy

create new, artificial opening

Example: tracheostomy (to create a new opening into the trachea)

-tome

instrument used to cut

Example: myotome (instrument used to cut muscle)

-tomy

process of cutting; incision

Example: tenotomy (process of cutting a tendon)
