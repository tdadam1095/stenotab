Obstetrics

Basics

  • Gynecology: medical-surgical specialty dealing with female reproductive system in non-pregnant state
  • Obstetrics: medical-surgical specialty dealing with female reproductive system in pregnant state
  • OB/GYN: combination of gynecology & obstetrics
  • Gravida: pregnant
    • Primigravida: woman who has been pregnant once
    • Multigravida: woman who has been pregnant more than once
  • Para: having given birth (regardless of whether stillbirth)
Gravidity and parity

Gravida 1, para 1 = woman who has been pregnant once & has given birth once

Gravida 3, para 2 = woman who has been pregnant 3 times & has given birth twice

  • Gestation period: length of pregnancy (about 40 weeks)
    • For the first 8 weeks following conception, the baby is called an embryo
    • After 8 week of pregnancy, the baby is called a fetus

Pregnancy

  • Placenta: organ that forms in the womb during pregnancy
    • Allows for the exchange of nutrients and waste products between the mother and the baby
    • Secretes the hormone human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG) at the start of pregnancy (detected by pregnancy tests)
    • Stimulates the release of estrogen and progesterone
    • Made of chorion (outer layer) and amnion (inner layer)
  • Umbilical cord: lifeline between the mother and baby
  • Amniotic sac: fluid-filled sac that protects the fetus

Childbirth

  • Hormones secreted by the baby trigger labor
  • Pull: the uterus contracts to dilate and pull the cervix over the baby's head
    • The hormone oxytocin controls the contractions
    • The amniotic sac opens ("water breaks") during labor
  • Push: the uterus pushes the baby down the birth canal
    • Pushing usually slows down as the baby's head crowns (emerges)
  • Shape-shift: the mother and baby accommodate to each other's shape
  • Turn: the baby spontaneously turns to allow delivery of the shoulders
    • Breech delivery: the baby is turned around, buttocks first; must c-section
  • Slide: the baby & placenta slide out
    • The umbilical cord gradually stops pulsing and can be safely cut a few minutes after delivery
  • Shrink: the uterus shrinks down to prevent blood loss
  • Apgar score: a rating to evaluate a newborn's health on a 2-point scale
    • A = activity/muscle tone
    • P = pulse
    • G = grimace/reflex
    • A = appearance/skin color
    • R = respiration

Post-Partum Period (Puerperium)

  • The uterus gradually returns to its pre-pregnancy size
  • Mammary glands are stimulated to lactate (produce milk)
  • Lasts 6-8 weeks

Pathologies

  • Ectopic pregnancy
    • Embryo implants outside the uterus (usually in fallopian tube)
    • The pregnancy is not viable
  • Hyperemesis gravidarum
    • Extreme morning sickness
    • Mother in danger of dehydration
    • Possible causes: rising blood levels of HCG
    • Treatment: IV fluids, anti-nausea meds, and avoiding triggers
  • Gestational diabetes
    • High blood sugar during pregnancy
    • Goes back to normal after birth
    • Manage with insulin
    • Baby will not be born with diabetes; increases the risk of an extremely large baby
  • Pregnancy-induced hypertension (pre-eclampsia)
    • Severe high blood pressure and swelling of hands, face, & feet
    • Cause: retention of toxins in mother's system
    • Can lead to convulsions in mother and problems for baby if untreated
  • Premature birth
    • A birth that takes place prior to 37 weeks' gestation
    • Babies may have low birth weight & jaundice and may spend time in the NICU
  • Shoulder Dystocia
    • Stuck shoulders
    • Baby fails to rotate to allow shoulders to ease out or baby is just too large for the mother's pelvis and gets stuck
    • Can cause excessive tearing of mother's tissues & bleeding, injury to baby's shoulder and brain damage (due to lack of oxygen)
  • Placenta previa
    • The placenta is abnormally positioned so that is covers the cervix
    • Blocks baby's exit from the womb
    • Requires surgical delivery of baby
  • Meconium aspiration syndrome
    • Meconium: baby's first feces
    • If meconium is passed in utero and the baby breathes it in during delivery, it can cause breathing issues for the baby
  • Fetal distress
    • Baby's heart rate becomes erratic or slows down

Procedures

  • Amniocentesis
    • A needle is used to withdraw a sample of amniotic fluid from around the fetus
    • Fluid is sent for genetic testing
  • Continuous fetal monitoring
    • If mother is anesthetized, she cannot cannot provide feedback about the progress of her labor or the baby’s condition
    • An electronic fetal monitor is strapped to the mother's abdomen
    • Electrodes may also be screwed into the baby's scalp
  • Assisted delivery
    • Forceps or a vacuum are applied to the baby’s head
    • The baby is manually pulled out of the birth canal
  • Episiotomy
    • The mother's perineum is cut
    • Obstetricians do this in complicated births (fetal distress, breech, shoulder dystocia, excessively large baby) and c-sections
      • Obstetricians also used to (and some still do) this routinely to “prevent tearing” – new research shows natural tears heal better
    • The incision must be surgically repaired
  • Cesarean section
    • Surgical delivery of baby
The "husband stitch"

A medically unnecessary and harmful surgical procedure in which one/more additional sutures than necessary are used to repair a woman's perineum after being torn during childbirth

Its purpose was to tighten the opening of the vagina for the pleasure of the woman's partner during intercourse

It can cause a lifetime of pain and is not an approved medical procedure

It is considered malpractice without the woman's consent in Canada
