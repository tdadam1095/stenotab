Muscular System

Functions

  • Voluntary muscle: under conscious control
  • Involuntary muscle: not under conscious control
  • 3 types of muscle tissue:
    • Visceral
      • Located in walls of internal organs
      • Under involuntary control
    • Cardiac
      • Located in heart
      • Under involuntary control
    • Skeletal
      • Located on top of bone
      • Under voluntary control
  • Ligaments attach bone to bone
  • Tendons attach muscle to bone
    • All skeletal muscles are connected to 2 bones
  • Muscles can only contract & relax; they work in pairs to accomplish a dual action
    • Flexion: bending a limb
    • Extension: straightening a limb
    • Hyperextension: overextending joint beyond anatomical position
    • Adduction: move toward midline
    • Abduction: move away from midline
    • Supination: turn palm forward
    • Pronation: turn palm backward
    • Eversion: move sole of foot outward away from midline
    • Inversion: move sole of foot inward toward midline
    • Dorsiflexion: bend foot upward
    • Plantar flexion: bend foot downward

Pathologies

  • Carpal tunnel syndrome
    • Tendons and ligaments in the small passageway in the wrist become inflamed when overused, which puts pressure on the median nerve
    • Symptoms: numbness, pain, tingling, and weakness
  • Muscular dystrophy (MD)
    • Degeneration of muscle tissue
    • Duchenne's muscular dystrophy is most common
    • Symptoms: muscular weakness; and loss of mobility, speech, and breathing
  • Strain (pulled muscle)
    • The tearing, twisting, overstretching, or overextension of muscles and tendons
    • Treatment: RICE (rest, ice, compression, elevation)
  • Sprain
    • Stretching of tearing of ligaments
    • Symptoms: swelling, inflammation, and bruising
  • Rotator cuff tendinitis
    • Inflammation of the rotator cuff (group of tendons that hold the shoulder joint in place)
    • Major cause: injury and degeneration
  • Fibromyalgia syndrome
    • Pain in fibrous tissues (muscles, tendons, and ligaments)
    • Associated with chronic fatigue syndrome
    • Symptoms: deep muscular aching; throbbing, burning, stabbing pain; and fatigue
    • Treatment: muscle relaxants, trigger point injections, physiotherapy, and acupuncture
