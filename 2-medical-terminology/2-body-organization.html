2 Body Organization

Body Cavities

  • Confined spaces within the body that contain internal organs
  • 2 major cavities which are further subdivided:
    • Dorsal cavity: posterior (back) surface of the body
      • Cranial cavity: contains the brain
      • Spinal cavity: contains the spinal cord (spinal canal)
    • Ventral cavity: anterior (front) surface of the body
      • Thoracic cavity: contains the heart & lungs
      • Abdominal cavity: contains the stomach, liver, kidneys, small intestines, & large intestine
      • Pelvic cavity: contains the urinary bladder, parts of the intestine, & some reproductive organs
        • Abdominopelvic cavity: refers to both the abdominal and pelvic cavities
        • Diaphragm: major respiratory muscle used for breathing that separates the thoracic and abdominopelvic cavities

Directional & Positional Terms

Standard Anatomical Position

  • Helps to identify accurate directional medical terms
    • Standing straight
    • Arms at side
    • Head forward
    • Palms forward
    • Feet forward

The midline

An imaginary line drawn down the center of the body from the top of the head to the feet

superior | cranial

above; toward the head

Example: head is superior to the neck

inferior | caudal

below; toward the lower end of the body or tail

Example: neck is inferior to the head

anterior | ventral

front surface of the body

Example: thoracic cavity is anterior to the spinal cavity

posterior | dorsal

back surface of the body

Example: spinal cavity is posterior to the thoracic cavity

medial

toward the midline

Example: big toe is medial to the small toe

lateral

away from the midline

Example: small toe is lateral to the big toe

proximal

nearest from the point of attachment to the trunk/point of origin

Example: stomach is proximal to the intestines

distal

farthest from the point of attachment to the trunk/point of origin

Example: intestines are distal to the stomach; stomach is distal to the throat

superficial

near the surface of the body

deep

away from the surface of the body

supine

lying on the back, face up (palms facing up)

prone

lying on the abdomen, face down (palms facing down)

plantar

sole of the foot

dorsum

upper portion of the foot
