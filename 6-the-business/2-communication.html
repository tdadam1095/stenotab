Communication

  • “Seek to understand before being understood.”
    • Steven Covey
  • Use empathic listening as a base character to inspire openness and trust,
  • Listen through their frame of reference.
  • Diagnose before you prescribe.
  • By judging first, you will never fully understand.

Keys to Communication

  • Maintain self-awareness
    • Be aware of emotions and motives
    • Are you being judgmental or too opinionated
  • Self-Regulation
    • Emotional stability and information processing in difficult situations. Anger, frustration, grief get in the way of effective communication. Use proper tone.
  • Empathy
    • Active listening
  • It is important to be receptive to feedback
  • Learn and adapt to change

Body Language

  • It is all the non-verbal communication we make – both conscious and unconscious.
  • Some studies suggest that only 7% of our judgments are based on what we actually say. These studies were usually taken when the body language was disagreeing with what was being said.
  • People believe far more by what they see than by what they hear.
  • You can change your body language to show empathy and to influence someone by slowing down your movements or taking away any unintended aggressive body language of your own.
  • You can learn to relax, calm and excite other people purely by the way you hold yourself, the way you move and the way you interact.
Aggressive body language
  • Hands on hips
  • Legs too wide
  • Invading personal space, too close
  • Aggressive gesturing (finger pointing)
  • Standing over someone
  • Overly firm handshake
  • Staring
Defensive body language
  • Crossed arms or legs
  • Hunched shoulders
  • Poor eye contact
  • Leaning away
  • Tight voice
Nervous body language
  • Nail biting
  • Dry throat (swallowing, coughing)
  • Blushing
  • Weak handshake
  • Avoiding eye contact
Bored body language
  • Looking around the room
  • Looking at watch
  • Drumming fingers
  • Yawning
  • Shifting weight
  • Rubbing face
Open & interested body language
  • Firm handshake
  • Good eye contact
  • On the same level
  • Confident stance
  • Confident, chosen gestures
  • Showing interest (head not, slight lean in)

Effective Oral Communication

  • Clear and concise expression of thoughts •Articulate, correct use of language, expressive
  • Makes good eye contact with others while speaking
  • Well-organized, complete, and courteous presentation of thoughts
  • Consistently holds the attention of others

The "Difficult Conversation"

  • Work begins before the conversation itself (mental preparation)
    • Courage, patience, confidence, vision success, reframe negatives
  • The approach
    • The manner in which you engage can tip the conversation to success.
    • Empathy: Seek to understand before being understood.
      • After you heard the other side, react with empathy, share information and your point of view, and turn the conversation back to the other person.
    • Collaboration, partnering, win/win
      • Don’t act like this is a competition or fight. Treat the conversation as a problem you are trying to work through.
    • Maintain their self-esteem and yours.
      • Speak and act like the other person is always feeling respected.
  • Build rapport
    • Stay adult
    • Build rapport
    • Manage tone of voice
    • Keep speed of speech slow, volume low
    • Take care of body language
  • What you say
    • No blame
    • Withhold judgment
    • Begin neutrally
    • Ask for more information or other's point of view
    • Respond by acknowledging their concerns
    • Share your point of view after hearing theirs
  • Power tools
    • Apologize and road repair
      • Apologize for whatever you contributed to a situation
      • Fix little potholes before they wreck relationships
    • Values and integrity
      • Let these guide your actions and your difficult conversations
    • Perception checking
