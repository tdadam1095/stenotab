Advisory Opinion 1 - Requesting an extension to accept freelance work

  • Reporter requests an extension to file a court transcript in order to accept freelance assignments
    • In violation of Provisions 6 and 9
No. 6

Refrain, as an official reporter, from freelance reporting activities that interfere with official duties and obligations.

No. 9

Maintain the integrity of the reporting profession.

Advisory Opinion 2 - Relationship with litigant

  • Reporter fails to disclose a relationship with Litigant
    • In violation of Provisions 1 and 2
No. 1

Be fair and impartial toward each participant in all aspects of reported proceedings, and always offer to provide comparable services to all parties in a proceeding.

No. 2

Be alert to situations that are conflicts of interest or that may give the appearance of a conflict of interest.  If a conflict or a potential conflict arises, the Member shall disclose that conflict or potential conflict.

Advisory Opinion 3 - Relationship with counsel

  • Reporter 1 requests Reporter 2 to cover a proceeding Reporter 1's spouse is conducting and keeps a commission
  • Reporter 1 covers when other members of their spouse's law firm schedule proceedings
  • Not in violation of Provisions 1 and 2 if:
    • Reporter 1 discloses their relationship
    • An agreement is reached on record by all counsel that there's no objection
    • Reporter 1's certificate conforms with this
No. 1

Be fair and impartial toward each participant in all aspects of reporting proceedings, and always offer to provide comparable services to all parties in a proceeding.

No. 2

Be alert to situations that are conflicts of interest or that may give the appearance of a conflict of interest. If a conflict or a potential conflict arises, the member shall disclose that conflict or potential conflict.

Advisory Opinion 4 - Altering/deleting portions of the record

  • After the conclusion of a trial
    • Judge tells Reporter to alter/delete portions of the record
      • Must confer with all counsel first, then change can be made
    • Counsel tells Reporter to change one of his remarks
      • Must be done by motion of the court
    • Counsel tells Reporter to change one of his client's statements
      • Must be done by agreement of all parties in writing or by ruling of the court
  • If not, Reporter is in violation of Provisions 1, 3, and 9
No. 1

Be fair and impartial toward each participant in all aspects of reporting proceedings, and always offer to provide comparable services to all parties in a proceeding.

No. 3

Guard against not only the fact but the appearance of impropriety.

No. 9

Maintain the integrity of the reporting profession.

Advisory Opinion 6 - On or off the record

  • Attorney 1 examines the witness then concludes the proceeding, but Attorney 2 does not agree and wants to examine the witness
  • Attorney 1, who hired Reporter, tells them to go off the record
  • Reporter goes off the record
    • In violation of Provisions 1 and 9
  • Attorney 1 and Attorney 2 arrive early and get into a heated argument related to the litigation
  • Attorney 1 asks to go on the record and Attorney 2 asks to stay off the record
  • Reporter does not go on the record
    • In violation of Provisions 1 and 4
No. 1

Be fair and impartial toward each participant in all aspects of reporting proceedings, and always offer to provide comparable services to all parties in a proceeding.

No. 4

Preserve the confidentiality and ensure the security of information, oral or written, entrusted to the Member by any of the parties in a proceeding.

No. 9

Maintain the integrity of the reporting profession.

Advisory Opinion 36 - Reporting where the witness appears by telephone

  • A notary in the presence of the witness appearing by telephone administers the oath instead of Reporter
    • If Reporter obtains proper documentation of administration of oath for attachment to the transcript, not a violation of Provision 5
No. 5

Be truthful and accurate when making public statements or when advertising the Member's qualifications or the services provided.

  • Witness appearing by telephone is not in the presence of a duly authorized oath giver
  • Attorneys want to waive the notarial requirement and stipulate that the oath given over the phone has the same effect as if administered in the presence of Reporter
  • Reporter does so
    • In violation of Provision 9 is not permissible by applicable law

Advisory Opinions 45 & 46 - Gift giving rules

  • Gift: any item/service with monetary value
  • Nominal gifts are allowed (e.g., pens or meals under $10)
  • Gifts exceeding $150 per recipient annually are not allowed (aggregate limit)
    • This excludes pro bono services
    • This excludes campaign donations to clients who decide to run for public office
    • This excludes gifts to clients who are also personal friends, but only if the gift-giving is reciprocal
  • Gifts in exchange for future work are not permitted
