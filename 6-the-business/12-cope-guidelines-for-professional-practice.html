COPE Guidelines for Professional Practice

This page was last updated October 2024

See full guidelines here: https://www.ncra.org/home/the-profession/NCRA-Code-of-Professional-Ethics/cope---guidelines-for-professional-practice

Section 1 - Court reporter

  • Accept appropriate assignments
    • Only take assignments where you can produce an accurate transcript - if your skills are inadequate, recommend another qualified reporter
  • Prepare the record properly
    • Follow transcript-preparation guidelines set by statute, court order, or local practices
  • Qualified substitutes
    • Ensure any substitute reporter is qualified for the assignment
  • Preserve notes
    • Keep shorthand notes for at least five years, either as original paper notes or electronic copies
  • Meet deadlines
    • Aim to deliver transcripts on time and notify clients of any delays
  • Continuous improvement
    • Stay proficient and up-to-date with current literature and technology; participate in continuing-education programs
  • Support the profession
    • Participate in activities that enhance the quality and standards of reporting
  • Cooperate with justice
    • Work with the legal community to improve the administration of justice
  • Pro bono services
    • Offer pro bono services when requested through qualified legal aid organizations, maintaining impartiality, competence, and integrity

Section 2 - Realtime reporter in legal proceedings

  • Clarify services needed
    • Determine who will require realtime services before starting
  • Use discretion in assignments
    • Accept assignments based on skill level and accurately represent your qualifications
  • Prepare in advance
    • Gather materials beforehand to create a job dictionary
  • Technical proficiency
    • Be knowledgeable about your software & hardware and able to troubleshoot basic issues
  • Ongoing education
    • Pursue further knowledge through workshops, meetings, and certification
  • Draft transcript disclaimer
    • Include a disclaimer with uncertified drafts stating they cannot be quoted or filed in court, and keep a copy for your records

Section 3 - Guidelines on providing uncertified draft transcripts

  • Aid justice
    • The primary goal is to assist in the administration of justice
  • Distinguish versions
    • Understand that there will be an unofficial and an official transcript for each proceeding; uncertified drafts may contain errors and cannot be filed with the court
  • Limit distribution
    • Uncertified transcripts should only go to the involved parties, not the public
  • Professional conduct
    • Maintain professionalism and comply with all relevant laws during the transcript preparation
  • Maintain impartiality
    • Avoid any situations that might compromise your neutrality or the accuracy of the record
  • Clearly mark drafts
    • Label each page as "uncertified draft transcript only" and avoid including elements that indicate finality
  • Handle untranslated content
    • Resolve any untranslated steno strokes before providing drafts, if possible
  • Minimum standards
    • Ensure that your writing skills meet the guidelines before distributing drafts, but you can provide them later if needed

Section 4 - Backup audio media

  • Ensure integrity
    • Comply with local, state, and federal laws regarding the integrity of the record
  • Understand "work product"
    • Recognize that backup recordings made at your discretion are your personal property and not public records
  • Providing backup audio media
    • Ensure no confidential discussions are included before sharing
    • Provide copies, not originals, unless ordered by a court
    • Offer the same access to all parties if sharing media
  • Offering as a value-added service
    • Inform all parties before proceedings start
    • Ensure recordings do not distort the record and contain no confidential discussions

Section 5 - Remote reporting guidelines

  • Logistical issues
    • Encourage all counsel to be on camera, including the reporter
    • Ensure everyone in the room is visible on camera
    • Avoid multiple microphones to prevent feedback
  • Reporting proceedings
    • Interrupt as needed to maintain an accurate record
    • Identify all participants clearly
    • If disconnection occurs, notify participants and request a repeat of missed statements
  • Transcript production
    • Clearly note that the proceeding was remote in the transcript
    • Use appropriate notations for technical difficulties and unidentified speakers
  • Recording considerations
    • Be designated as host to prevent unauthorized recordings
    • Remind all parties that recording is not allowed unless consented
