Types of Law

The Law

  • A set of rules created by a society that governs the conduct of its members, is recognized and enforced by the government, and includes a punishment if broken
  • The rule of law:
    • No one is above the law; everyone is subject to the law and treated equally
    • The court system settles disputes; we don't take matters into our own hands

1. Substantive Law

  • Tells you what the rules are
  1. Public law deals with issues that affect society as a whole
  2. Private law deals with issues that affect private individuals or organizations

2. Procedural Law

  • Tells you how to apply or enforce those rules.
Substantive vs. procedural

Substantive: You are charged with theft

Procedural:

  • Police must have reasonable grounds to search and arrest you
  • Police must inform you of your legal rights and give you a chance to contact counsel
  • Police must file a charge specifying what you are accused of
  • Police must release you or bring you before a judge or justice of the peace in order to speak to bail
  • The trial must occur within a reasonable timeframe
  • The trial must adhere to certain procedures and strict rules of evidence
