Presentation of Evidence

Fundamental Principles of Justice

  1. The accused is presumed innocent
  2. Crown has the burden of proof
    So...
  • Crown must present their case first
    • Call witnesses to testify
    • Enter exhibits
  • Accused has the option to call a case (they don't have to prove anything)
  • Formal commitment to tell the truth should be binding upon one's conscience
    • Oath: includes a statement to a high power based upon one's religion
    • Affirmation: does not include a religious statement

Presentation & Purpose of Testimony

  • Cross-examination is limited to issues arising from examination-in-chief (direct examination)
  • Re-examination is limited to issues arising from cross-examination
  • Re-cross-examination is limited to issues arising from re-examination
  • Leading question: a question which suggests the answer
    • Only permitted in cross-examination/re-cross examination
A leading question

Q What color was the vehicle?
vs.
Q Was the vehicle a black Ford SUV?

Exhibits

  • Tangible items
    • Put before the trier of fact
    • To be considered in arriving at a verdict
  • Exhibits for identification
    • Not completely identified (who, what, where) and/or
    • Continuity has not been established
  • Full exhibit
    • Completely identified (who, what, where) and
    • Continuity has been established (unbroken chain of custody/proper preservation)
  • Exhibits for identification
  • Only full exhibits can be relied upon (considered) by the trier of fact
