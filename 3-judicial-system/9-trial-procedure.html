9 Trial Procedure

Defence chose not to call evidence - who argues first?

Crown would argue first

  • Opening statements are atypical because the judiciary is capable of following the evidence and discerning the relevant information
    • Only made when the case is particularly complex or complicated
  • The accused is usually defence's first witness
    • Because they must be present in the courtroom and cannot be subject to an order excluding witnesses (avoids influence from hearing other defence witnesses)
    • The accused is not required to testify if defence calls evidence
  • Defence is not requited to disclose their evidence to Crown even if they intend to call evidence except:
    • Expert witness
    • Alibi

Rebuttal Evidence

  • Separate category of evidence presented by Crown to refute or disprove defence evidence
  • Must be new evidence
  • Presented with permission of the Court

Surrebuttal Evidence

  • Separate category of evidence presented by defence to refute or disprove rebuttal evidence of the Crown
  • Presented with permission of the Court
