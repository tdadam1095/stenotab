Provincial Court of Alberta Divisions

Criminal Division

  • Judges preside over:
    • Docket courts
      • Courts of first appearance
      • Pleas, bails
    • Trials
    • Preliminary inquiries: pretrial hearing held in Provincial Court to determine whether there's enough evidence to proceed to trial in Court of King's Bench
      • At its conclusion, the accused is either ordered to stand trial or discharged
      • Sometimes, the Crown will proceed by direct Indictment (skipping preliminary inquiry)
        • Requires written consent of the Attorney General or
        • The accused may waive their right to a preliminary inquiry before or during the proceedings
    • Fatality inquiries: hearing to inquire into circumstances surrounding a death & make recommendations to prevent similar deaths in the future

Civil Division

  • Judges preside over:
    • "Small claims" court
      • For minor legal disputes
      • Often self-represented litigants
      • $50,000 limit (as of August 1, 2024 - up from $25,000)

Family Division

  • Judges preside over:
    • Custody, access, maintenance
    • Emergency Protection Orders (EPOs)
    • Apprehension orders
    • Temporary/permanent guardianship
  • All proceedings held in camera

Youth Division

  • Judges preside over:
    • Criminal trials for young offenders (ages 12-17)
      • Charges under the Criminal Code of Canada
        • Same offences as adults
      • Trial guided by the Youth Criminal Justice Act
        • Procedural protections for young offenders
        • Additional sentencing options

Traffic Division

  • Justices of the Peace preside over:
    • Trials arising from provincial statues and municipal bylaws
      • Animal
      • Parking
      • Litter
      • Gaming
      • Liquor

Alberta Court of King's Bench

  • Justices preside over:
    • Criminal and civil trials
    • Jury trials
    • Some appeals from Provincial Court
  • Sits in judicial centres

Alberta Court of Appeal

  • Justices preside over:
    • Appeals from the Court of Queen's Bench and the Provincial Court
      • Criminal and civil
      • No viva voce evidence (or at least extremely rare)
    • Typically a panel of 3
      • QB justices can sit ex officio
  • Sits in Edmonton or Calgary

Sitting Points in Alberta

  • 11 judicial centres in 11 judicial districts
    • Regular Court of Queen's Bench
    • Regular Provincial Court
  • 20+ base points
    • Regular Provincial Court (daily sessions)
  • 50+ circuit points
    • Intermittent Provincial Court (judges will travel here on an intermittent basis)-
  • For all locations, see here: https://albertacourts.ca/cj/court-practice-and-schedules/locations-map

Supreme Court of Canada

  • Justices preside over:
    • Appeals from provincial and territorial Courts of Appeal
      • Criminal & civil
      • No viva voce evidence
    • Typically a panel of 7 or 9
  • Sits in Ottawa
